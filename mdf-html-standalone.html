Choose Your SPIF Target
Work with your FranklinWH Sales Representative to select the best option for your market. Each uses a credit memo — no cash or gift cards.
A
Distributor SPIF
SPIF goes to the distributor based on units sold.
How it works:
Distributor tracks equipment sold in real time against MDF-funded SPIF targets. Once the SPIF period ends or MDF allocation is exhausted, the contest closes.
Why choose this:
Easiest to track — distributors have real-time visibility into POs and units moved. Recommended starting point.
B
FranklinWH Certified Installer SPIF
SPIF goes to the FranklinWH Certified Installer putting product in homes.
How it works:
Distributor reports FranklinWH Certified Installer-level sales data. FranklinWH issues a credit memo to the distributor, who passes the credit through to qualifying FranklinWH Certified Installers.
Why choose this:
Targets the person physically moving the product. Requires distributor cooperation on FranklinWH Certified Installer-level tracking.
C
Homeowner Incentive
Savings passed to the homeowner via reduced project cost.
How it works:
FranklinWH issues a credit memo to the distributor. Distributor reduces cost to the FranklinWH Certified Installer. FranklinWH Certified Installer passes savings to the homeowner as a lower project price. No gift cards, no cash — just a reduced price.
Why choose this:
Makes FranklinWH more competitive at the point of sale and gives homeowners more savings.
How the money flows: FranklinWH issues a credit memo to the distributor — distributor applies credit toward FranklinWH Certified Installer cost — savings passed through the chain. No money exchanging hands directly. SPIF amounts are flexible — work with your FranklinWH Sales Representative to set per-unit amounts that stretch your MDF allocation across a meaningful contest duration (2–3 months recommended).
⚠ Key Requirement
All requests require FranklinWH marketing team review. A clearly defined sales-linked KPI is mandatory — requests without one are automatically declined

Units sold during SPIF period, winner details, sales uplift data, ROI calculation

Which of the 5 categories fits? For SPIFs, also choose Option A, B, or C.

Does your idea appear in the Qualifies list? Is anything in the Does Not Qualify list?

Use these scenarios to gut-check your request before submitting.

Event — APPROVED
SCENARIO
Distributor hosts a regional FranklinWH Certified Installer summit with FranklinWH product training, booth space, materials, and lunch for 30 reps.
WHY
Training agenda, FranklinWH co-branding, measurable attendees, expected pipeline generation.
DECISION
Approved. Requires sign-in sheet, branded photos, and follow-up lead report within 60 days.
Event — REJECTED
SCENARIO
Distributor wants to sponsor a sporting event as a community goodwill gesture.
WHY
No FranklinWH branding, no training agenda, no pipeline generation metrics.
DECISION
Convert to a branded FranklinWH event with messaging and a formal training component.
Swag + SPIF — APPROVED
SCENARIO
Distributor plans to use $2–4K in MDF toward a sales SPIF for their top 4 accounts, plus branded hats for the participants.
WHY
Clear sales KPI (top account activation), defined audience (4 accounts), SPIF + collateral working together.
DECISION
Approved. Requires SPIF results reporting, hat distribution log with recipient details, and post-event lead report.
Swag — REJECTED
SCENARIO
Distributor requests FranklinWH hats to have on hand for general customer meetings.
WHY
No sales KPI, no target event, no measurable outcome. This is inventory building. Hats must be attached to a specific initiative.
DECISION
Tie the hats to a specific event, training, or SPIF with a measurable sales target (e.g., "50 hats for FranklinWH Certified Installer kickoff targeting 5 new accounts").
