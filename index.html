Sales Incentives & SPIFs
Sales Performance Incentive Fund (SPIF) programs — choose distributor or FranklinWH Certified Installer

QUALIFIES

•SPIF programs (2–3 month duration recommended)
•Tiered rewards: base per-unit + bonuses for top performers
•Pre-approved branded prizes and participation raffles
•Inventory relief SPIFs to help move product post-price changes

DOES NOT QUALIFY

•Cash rewards or gift cards of any kind
•Non-branded gifts over $50 USD
•Incentives resulting in non-FranklinWH system sales
•Using MDF credits to purchase more FranklinWH product
•Swag without a defined, measurable sales KPI
Choose Your SPIF Target
Work with your FranklinWH Sales Representative to select the best option for your market. Each uses a credit memo — no cash or gift cards.
A
Distributor SPIF
SPIF goes to distributor sales reps based on units sold.
How it works:
Distributor tracks equipment sold in real time against MDF-funded SPIF targets. Once the SPIF period ends or MDF allocation is exhausted, the contest closes.
Why choose this:
Easiest to track — distributors have real-time visibility into POs and units moved. Recommended starting point.
B
FranklinWH Certified Installer SPIF
SPIF goes to the FranklinWH Certified Installer putting product in homes.
How it works:
Distributor reports FranklinWH Certified Installer-level sales data. FranklinWH issues a credit memo to the distributor, who passes the credit through to qualifying FranklinWH Certified Installers.
Why choose this:
Targets the person physically moving the product. These are the people who drive consumer branding and loyalty in local markets.
How the money flows: FranklinWH issues a credit memo to the distributor — distributor applies credit toward FranklinWH Certified Installer cost — savings passed through the chain. No money exchanging hands directly. MDF credit memos cannot be used to purchase more FranklinWH product.

Budget Playbooks

Not sure where to start? These sample allocations show how to approach MDF at different budget levels. These are recommendations — work with your FranklinWH Sales Representative to tailor for your market.

$2,500Smaller MDF Budget

With a smaller allocation, focus your spend on one or two high-impact categories rather than spreading thin. A SPIF may not stretch far enough to run a meaningful contest — consider whether collateral, a targeted training event, or a demo installation would generate more value.

Option A: Focused SPIF80% SPIF / 20% Collateral
Run a short SPIF at $250/battery targeting 2–3 top installer accounts. Use remaining funds for branded hats or materials to support the effort. Expect ~8 units to exhaust the SPIF budget.
Option B: Training + Collateral60% Event / 40% Collateral
Host a half-day product training for a targeted group of installers. Use remaining funds for printed materials and co-branded leave-behinds. Best for markets where product education is the bottleneck.
Option C: Demo Installation100% Solution Wall
Install a FranklinWH demo unit in a PC storefront or installer showroom. High long-term visibility with minimal ongoing cost. Best for locations with high foot traffic.
$30,000Larger MDF Budget

With a larger allocation, diversify across categories for sustained impact. Lead with SPIFs to drive near-term sales, layer in events for pipeline development, and use collateral and digital to support brand presence. Work with your FranklinWH Sales Representative to build a quarterly plan.

Suggested Allocation50% SPIF / 30% Events / 10% Digital / 10% Collateral
$15K toward a 2–3 month tiered SPIF across your top installer accounts ($250–$500/battery + top performer bonuses). $9K toward regional training summits or product bootcamps. $3K toward co-branded digital campaigns. $3K toward printed materials, one-pagers, and event collateral.
Aggressive Sales Push80% SPIF / 20% Events
If moving inventory is the priority — for example, after a price adjustment — allocate the majority to SPIFs. Run a higher per-unit incentive ($500–$750/battery) to accelerate sell-through. Pair with a kickoff event to build momentum. This approach works short-term but may get stale if repeated quarterly.
Balanced Brand + Sales40% SPIF / 25% Events / 20% Digital / 15% Collateral
Best for markets where installer education and consumer brand awareness are equally important. Spread the investment across a moderate SPIF, installer training events, localized digital campaigns, and professional collateral to build long-term market presence.
